STYLE 01 — CINEMATIC BANNER Black &amp; White Documentary Single grayscale photograph, blood-red headline banner across the midline 1. Design identity This style evokes a documentary poster or vintage magazine feature. A single full-bleed, high-contrast black-and-white photograph carries the entire visual weight. A bold, slightly distressed serif or rough-sans headline in deep red sits across the middle of the frame, partially overlapping the subjects. The feeling is investigative, serious, and historical. Use this style for: deep-dive features, documentary-style episodes, historical retrospectives, &apos;the untold story of&apos; content, behind-the-scenes explorations. &quot;One strong photograph. One loud red line. Everything else gets out of the way.&quot; 2. Canvas specifications Attribute Value Dimensions 1280 × 720 pixels Aspect ratio 16:9 Color mode RGB / sRGB File format JPG, under 2 MB Safe zone Keep key subjects clear of bottom 60 px (timestamp overlay) 3. Photography treatment The photograph is 90% of this design. Choose it carefully and grade it ruthlessly. 3.1 Image selection Pick a single wide or horizontal photograph that fills the full 1280 × 720 canvas edge to edge. No borders, no matting, no background color showing. Ideal images have real environmental depth — a person at a mixing desk, a band in a studio, a crowd scene. Studio portraits on a plain backdrop will look flat in this treatment. Expressive faces work best. The sample uses a wide-eyed, shouting subject — emotion carries the click. Avoid images where the most interesting element sits dead-center on the horizontal midline, because the red banner will cover it. 3.2 Black &amp; white conversion Desaturate the image completely (saturation = 0). Push contrast HARD — blacks should be near pure black (RGB 5–15), whites near pure white (RGB 240–250). Lift midtone contrast so faces and textures pop. The look is editorial, not moody. Optional: add a very subtle warm or sepia tint (≤5% saturation) to soften the pure grayscale feel. Add light film grain (4–6% opacity) across the whole image for texture. 3.3 What makes it work Raw, unposed feeling — candid moments over formal portraits. Deep shadows with visible detail (not crushed into pure black). A documentary, slightly imperfect quality — don&apos;t over-retouch faces or clean up backgrounds. 4. Headline treatment Copy is provided by the copywriter — your job is to set it. These rules govern how the provided text is rendered, not what it says. 4.1 Typography specs Property Specification Typeface Heavy condensed sans with slight roughness: Anton, Bebas Neue, Druk Wide, or a rough-edged display face Weight Black (900) or Heavy (800) Case ALL CAPS, always Color Deep blood red (#A32D2D to #B8322E range) Size Cap height approximately 80–95 px per line Lines Usually 2 lines, stacked tightly (line-height ≈ 0.9) Letter spacing Slightly tight: −5 to −15 tracking units Alignment Left-aligned, starting ~40–60 px from left edge Vertical position Centered vertically on canvas midline (y ≈ 220–440 for 2-line headline) Texture Optional: add a subtle rough/distressed overlay at 10–15% opacity on the text for a worn-poster feel 4.2 Placing provided copy The copywriter will hand you a headline. Your placement rules: Short copy (1–2 words): Render as a single line, larger cap height (~110–130 px), still left-aligned on the midline. Medium copy (3–5 words): Break into 2 stacked lines. Pick a break that keeps line 1 and line 2 roughly equal in visual width. Example: &apos;THE MADNESS / BEHIND MAGIC&apos; not &apos;THE / MADNESS BEHIND MAGIC&apos;. Long copy (6+ words): Ask the copywriter to shorten it. This style breaks down beyond 2 lines — it becomes a wall of red and loses impact. Never add your own words (punctuation, sub-headlines, episode numbers) unless the copywriter supplies them. 4.3 Headline positioning Position the headline so it covers the upper torso / lower-face region of the subject(s). It should crop into the photograph, not sit in empty space above or below it. The goal: the red text visually &apos;cuts through&apos; the photograph. If your headline sits entirely in sky or empty backdrop, reposition the photo or the text. Leave at least 40 px of clear space between the headline and the left edge. Don&apos;t let letters touch the canvas edge. 5. Color system Role Sample Hex Use Photo blacks #0A0A0A Crushed shadows in photo Photo whites #F4F4F4 Highlights in photo Headline red #A32D2D Primary headline color Alt headline red #B8322E Slightly brighter alternative Only three colors live in this design: black, white, and one red. Do not introduce a second hue. If the brief provides a hook color other than red, substitute it in place of the red but keep the same saturation and depth (deep, cinematic, not candy-bright). 6. Step-by-step build Step 1 — Set up canvas Create a 1280 × 720 px document, RGB, 72+ DPI. Step 2 — Place and grade the photograph Import the chosen photograph and scale to fill the canvas edge to edge. Convert to black and white (desaturate to 0). Increase contrast aggressively — crush blacks, protect whites. Add a subtle film-grain layer at 4–6% opacity. Step 3 — Set the provided headline copy Create a text layer with the copy supplied by the copywriter. Apply the typographic specs from section 4.1. Break across 1–2 lines per the rules in section 4.2 — never invent or edit the words. Position left-aligned, vertically centered on the midline, cutting through the subject(s). Step 4 — Optional distress pass Apply a rough/distressed texture mask to the text at 10–15% opacity to add a worn, documentary feel. Skip this if the provided copy already uses a distressed display font. Step 5 — Review at thumbnail size Scale to 240 × 135 px and check: is the headline legible? Is the subject&apos;s face still readable behind the text? Step 6 — Export Export as JPG, quality 85–92, under 2 MB. 7. Quick do / don&apos;t reference DO DON&apos;T Use one full-bleed photograph Use collages, multiple panels, or cutouts Convert to true high-contrast B&amp;W Leave color in the photo Position text so it crops into the subject Float text in empty sky or negative space Use one red, one black, one white — no more Introduce secondary accent colors Render the copy exactly as supplied Edit, reorder, or punctuate the provided copy Keep to 1–2 lines of headline text Let text wrap to 3+ lines (ask for shorter copy) Use ALL CAPS heavy condensed type Use script, serif, or light-weight fonts 8. Pre-publish checklist ☐ Canvas is 1280 × 720 px ☐ Single photograph fills entire canvas, no borders ☐ Photo is converted to high-contrast B&amp;W with crushed blacks ☐ Subtle film grain has been applied ☐ Headline copy matches what the copywriter supplied, word for word ☐ Headline is heavy condensed sans, ALL CAPS, deep red ☐ Headline is positioned to cut through the subject(s) on the midline ☐ Line break keeps both lines roughly equal width ☐ Only three colors present: black, white, red ☐ Tested at 240 × 135 px — still legible ☐ Exported as JPG under 2 MB End of template guide One photo. One red line. Nothing else.
